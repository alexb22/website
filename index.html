Survey Reader

The Survey Reader was a project I completed during one of my Data Structures and Algorithms courses in college. The entire class took a survey that first asked a series of personal questions including what your major is, where you're from, and what your favorite hobby is. Next, you were presented a list of songs and you had to answer whether you have heard the song and whether you like the song. The application parsed the .csv output of the survey, stored the information in various data structures including a customly implemented linked list, and displayed the data in a user friendly UI.

The UI buttons called a selection sort algorithm to sort the results based on artist name, song title, release year, or genre. Additionally, the user could represent the results by major, favorite hobby, or region.
